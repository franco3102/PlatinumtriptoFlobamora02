About Flobamora :

Flobamora is an abbreviation for the archipelago in Indonesia, with 3 main islands, Flores Island, Sumba Island, and Timor Island flanked by small islands such as Sabu Island, Rote Island, Semau Island, Alor Island, and others. The group of islands, in the administrative territories of Indonesia's geopolitical and administrative territories since 1958, were combined in the provincial region known as East Nusa Tenggara.

Some interesting picture in Flobamora :

1. Nembrala Beach in Rote

Nembrala beach is touted as the Kuta of Rote Island with white sand. Nembrala Beach is quite satisfying the eyes with its blue sea water crashing aside to the white sand of its long beach. Nembrala Beach has waves on an international scale so it's no wonder many foreign tourists visit it. Even in August to October, the waves on this beach can reach a height of 7 meters.

2. Kelimutu Lake Ende, Flores

Kelimutu Lake is located in Pemo Village, Kelimutu District, Ende Regency, East Nusa Tenggara. The name Kelimutu itself comes from the word "Keli" which means mountain and "Mutu" which means boiling. Lake Kelimutu, also known as the three-color lake, offers a natural view that pampers the eyes of every tourist who visits with its colorful water sheen. For those of you who like to enjoy the sunrise can visit Lake Kelimutu.

3. Komodo and padar Island Labuhan Bajo, Flores

First picture is Komodo Island. Komodo Island is an island located in the Nusa Tenggara Islands. Komodo Island is known as a habitat for native Komodo dragons. This island is also the Komodo National Park area which is managed by the Central Government. On Komodo Island, Komodo dragons live and breed well. Until August 2009, on this island there were around 1300 Komodo dragons.

Second Picture is Padar Island. Padar Island is the third largest island in the Komodo National Park Area. Compared to Rinca Island, Padar Island is indeed known by the public late. However, this island is still a very exotic island in Indonesia. The topography of the island is steep with volcanic mountains in steep seas and hills facing deep sea bays. Padar has a dry climate with bushes and grassland trees covering the island, creating beautiful savannah hill conditions.

4. Koka Beach in Maumere, Flores

Koka Beach Maumere is located in Wolowiro Village, Paga District, Sikka Regency, Flores, East Nusa Tenggara. The distance itself is approximately 48 km from the center of Maumere City. Koka Beach is often referred to as the dream beach. Many also say it is a hidden paradise. This is because of the beauty it has. White sand beaches, clear sea water with gradations of colors from light blue to very clear dark blue, clear coral reefs, and the expanse of small islands around it makes this place very perfect.

5. Weekuri Lake in Sumba Island

Lake Weekuri is located in Kalenarogo Village, North Kodi District, Southwest Sumba Regency, East Nusa Tenggara Province. This bluish green Lake Weekuri is a lake that is very unique compared to other lakes in general in Indonesia because of its salty water content and brackish. Why is it salty? Because this lake is actually a lagoon that is formed from open ocean water in the lake range. Lake Weekuri has very clear water with a water depth that is not deep enough, so it is suitable for visitors to just play with water and swim.

Bukit Tanarara is a very instagramable photo spot in Katiku Luku, Matawai La Pawu, East Sumba Regency, NTT. This Tanarara Hill at a glance means red earth, which is taken from the color of the soil on a red hill. Besides this Tanarara Hill has another uniqueness that is a hill that is almost the entire surface covered by grass. Bends of hills that add neat views make you more comfortable in this place.
